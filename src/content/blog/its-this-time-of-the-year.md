For many of us, *it's this time of the year*, when we need to stop, reflect on the past couple of months, gather our notes, make peace with our past and write down our performance assessments. Personally, this is the most challenging year of my career; both due to the amount of the reports I have, and due to starting to understand what my new role entails and how should I approach it.

Nepotism aside, the majority of people have their first contact with *management*, when they land their first job in an organization. There's someone that takes care of certain things, such as shifts, priorities, what needs to be done, setting policy, giving praise and constructive feedback. No matter how good or bad this person is, they tend to have a special place in our heart: One with mostly negative adjectives to describe their character, motivation, agenta, behavior... The list can go on indefinately.

And yet, there they are, having a specific position within the organization, performing a job &mdash; much like we all do &mdash; to provide sustanance to themselves and their families, and being evaluated, as you are evaluated.

*Have I been there?*, you may ask and I'd tell you that I've been there countless times, judging, critisizing, condemning what I could not see nor understand. In fact, a couple of years ago, while discussing with my manager, I confided to him that I wouldn't want to be in his position. As ironic as it may sound now that I'm there, it was a conscious choice. 

*Have I completely understood what needs to be done?*

Let's find out
